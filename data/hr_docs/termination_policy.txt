Termination and Separation Policy

Types of Termination:

1. Voluntary Resignation:
- Employee-initiated separation
- Notice period required (2 weeks minimum)
- Exit interview conducted
- Final pay and benefits processed
- Knowledge transfer expected

2. Involuntary Termination:
- Performance-based termination
- Policy violation termination
- Position elimination
- Company restructuring
- At-will employment termination

3. Layoffs:
- Business necessity
- Position elimination
- Department restructuring
- Economic conditions
- Severance package provided

Notice Periods:
- Employees: 2 weeks notice requested
- Company: 2 weeks notice or pay in lieu
- Executive positions: 30 days notice
- Contract positions: Per contract terms

Exit Process:

1. Resignation Submission:
- Written notice to manager and HR
- Effective date specified
- Reason for leaving (optional)
- Transition plan discussed

2. Exit Interview:
- Conducted by HR
- Feedback collected
- Company property returned
- Final questions answered
- Benefits information provided

3. Knowledge Transfer:
- Document current projects
- Train replacement or team
- Hand off responsibilities
- Share access credentials
- Complete pending tasks

4. Final Pay:
- Accrued vacation paid out
- Final paycheck issued
- Benefits continuation information
- COBRA information provided
- 401(k) rollover options

Termination for Cause:
- Policy violations
- Performance issues
- Misconduct
- Theft or fraud
- Insubordination

Progressive Discipline:
- Verbal warning
- Written warning
- Final warning
- Suspension (if applicable)
- Termination

Severance Packages:
- Based on years of service
- Typically 1-2 weeks per year
- Health insurance continuation
- Outplacement services
- Non-compete considerations

Non-Compete Agreements:
- May apply to certain positions
- Duration and scope specified
- Geographic limitations
- Industry restrictions
- Enforceability varies by state

Return of Company Property:
- Laptops and equipment
- Access cards and badges
- Company credit cards
- Documents and files
- Software and licenses

Access Revocation:
- Immediate upon termination
- Email and systems access
- Building access revoked
- Network access disabled
- All accounts deactivated

Final Paycheck:
- Issued on next regular payday
- Accrued vacation included
- Unused sick leave not paid
- Deductions applied
- Tax withholdings processed

Benefits Continuation:
- COBRA health insurance available
- 18-36 months continuation
- Employee pays full premium
- Information provided within 14 days
- Election period of 60 days

References:
- Employment verification provided
- Dates of employment confirmed
- Position and title verified
- Rehire eligibility status
- Performance details (with consent)

Rehire Eligibility:
- Eligible: Voluntary resignation in good standing
- Not eligible: Termination for cause
- Case-by-case review
- Previous performance considered
- Business needs evaluated

Unemployment Benefits:
- Company provides required information
- State determines eligibility
- Appeals process available
- No company involvement in decisions
- Legal compliance maintained
