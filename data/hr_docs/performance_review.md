# Performance Review Process

## Annual Review Cycle

All employees participate in an annual performance review process. The review period runs from January 1st to December 31st, with reviews conducted in January of the following year.

## Review Components

### 1. Self-Assessment
- Employees complete a self-assessment form
- Reflect on achievements, challenges, and goals
- Submit 2 weeks before review meeting

### 2. Manager Review
- Managers evaluate employee performance
- Review goals from previous year
- Assess competencies and behaviors
- Provide written feedback

### 3. 360-Degree Feedback (Optional)
- For leadership positions, peer feedback may be collected
- Direct reports provide input for managers
- Cross-functional team members may be included

### 4. Review Meeting
- One-on-one meeting between employee and manager
- Discuss performance, feedback, and development
- Set goals for upcoming year
- Document action items

## Performance Ratings

- **Exceeds Expectations**: Exceptional performance, consistently exceeds goals
- **Meets Expectations**: Solid performance, meets all goals
- **Needs Improvement**: Performance below expectations, improvement plan required
- **Unsatisfactory**: Significant performance issues, formal improvement plan

## Goal Setting

### SMART Goals Framework
- **Specific**: Clear and well-defined objectives
- **Measurable**: Quantifiable success criteria
- **Achievable**: Realistic and attainable
- **Relevant**: Aligned with company and team objectives
- **Time-bound**: Clear deadlines and milestones

### Goal Categories
1. Business Objectives: Revenue, efficiency, quality metrics
2. Professional Development: Skills, certifications, growth
3. Leadership: Team development, mentoring, influence
4. Innovation: Process improvements, new initiatives

## Mid-Year Check-ins

Quarterly check-ins are recommended to:
- Review progress on goals
- Address challenges early
- Adjust goals if needed
- Maintain ongoing communication

## Performance Improvement Plans (PIP)

If performance is below expectations:
1. Manager documents specific issues
2. HR reviews and approves PIP
3. Clear improvement objectives set
4. Regular check-ins scheduled
5. Timeline typically 60-90 days
6. Outcome: improvement or separation

## Compensation Reviews

Performance reviews inform:
- Annual salary adjustments
- Bonus eligibility
- Promotion considerations
- Development opportunities

## Performance Improvement Process

### Identifying Performance Issues:
- Regular monitoring of performance metrics
- Feedback from multiple sources
- Documentation of specific concerns
- Pattern recognition over time
- Comparison to role expectations

### Improvement Plan Development:
- Clear, specific objectives
- Measurable success criteria
- Realistic timelines
- Required resources identified
- Support mechanisms in place
- Regular check-in schedule

### Support During Improvement:
- Manager coaching and guidance
- Training and development resources
- Peer mentoring opportunities
- HR consultation available
- Regular progress reviews
- Adjustment of plan if needed

### Success Factors:
- Employee commitment to improvement
- Clear communication
- Realistic expectations
- Adequate support provided
- Regular feedback
- Timely intervention

## Performance Documentation

### What Gets Documented:
- Performance goals and objectives
- Regular feedback and discussions
- Achievement of milestones
- Areas for improvement
- Training completed
- Recognition received

### Documentation Standards:
- Objective and factual
- Specific examples provided
- Timely documentation
- Employee acknowledgment
- Secure storage
- Retention policies followed

## Performance Review Best Practices

### For Managers:
- Prepare thoroughly
- Provide specific examples
- Focus on behavior and results
- Listen actively
- Be fair and consistent
- Support development

### For Employees:
- Come prepared with examples
- Be open to feedback
- Ask clarifying questions
- Discuss development goals
- Share accomplishments
- Request support when needed

## Performance Review Appeals

### Appeal Process:
- Submit written appeal within 10 days
- Include supporting documentation
- Review by higher management
- Independent assessment if needed
- Final decision communicated
- Documentation updated

### Appeal Grounds:
- Factual errors in review
- Procedural violations
- Bias or discrimination
- Incomplete information
- Unfair assessment
- Retaliation concerns
