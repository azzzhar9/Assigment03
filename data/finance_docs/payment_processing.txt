Payment Processing Procedures

This document outlines procedures for processing various types of payments.

Payment Types:

1. Vendor Payments:
- Processed through Accounts Payable
- Based on approved invoices
- Follow payment terms (Net 30, etc.)
- Weekly payment runs

2. Employee Expenses:
- Reimbursed via expense reports
- Processed within 10 days of approval
- Paid via payroll direct deposit
- See Expense Policy for details

3. Payroll:
- Bi-weekly payroll processing
- Direct deposit to employee accounts
- Pay stubs available in HR portal
- Tax withholdings as per employee elections

4. Contractor Payments:
- Based on approved invoices or timesheets
- 1099 issued for amounts over $600/year
- Processed similar to vendor payments
- Requires contractor agreement on file

Payment Methods:

ACH Transfer:
- Preferred method for regular payments
- Lower cost and faster processing
- Requires vendor banking information
- Typically 1-2 business days

Wire Transfer:
- For large amounts or urgent payments
- International wire transfers available
- Higher fees apply
- Same-day or next-day processing

Check:
- For vendors without electronic payment
- Mailed to vendor address on file
- Requires check stock and signature
- 3-5 business days for delivery

Credit Card:
- For small purchases or subscriptions
- Company credit cards available
- Monthly reconciliation required
- Immediate payment

Payment Schedule:

Regular Payments:
- Weekly: Every Tuesday
- Cut-off: Previous Friday 5 PM
- Processed: Tuesday morning
- Received: Wednesday-Thursday

Urgent Payments:
- Same-day processing available
- Requires manager and Finance approval
- Additional fees may apply
- Use only for true emergencies

International Payments:
- Additional 2-3 business days
- Currency conversion at time of payment
- Bank fees may apply
- Requires additional information

Payment Authorization:

Approval Requirements:
- All payments require appropriate approval
- Approval levels based on amount
- Must match approved budget
- Invoice must be approved

Payment Limits:
- Under $1,000: Manager approval
- $1,000-$5,000: Department head
- $5,000-$25,000: CFO
- Over $25,000: CEO

Dual Authorization:
- Required for payments over $10,000
- Two authorized signers
- Prevents fraud and errors
- Additional security measure

Payment Processing Steps:

1. Invoice Received:
- Logged in accounting system
- Assigned invoice number
- Date stamped

2. Verification:
- Match to purchase order (if applicable)
- Verify amounts and calculations
- Check for duplicates
- Confirm approval

3. Approval:
- Route to appropriate approver
- Approver reviews and approves
- Rejected invoices returned with reason

4. Payment Setup:
- Select payment method
- Enter payment details
- Schedule payment date
- Generate payment file

5. Execution:
- Process payment file
- Send to bank or print check
- Record in accounting system
- Generate confirmation

6. Reconciliation:
- Match bank statement
- Verify all payments cleared
- Investigate discrepancies
- Update records

Payment Issues:

Failed Payments:
- Insufficient funds (rare)
- Incorrect banking information
- Bank rejection
- Contact vendor immediately

Payment Disputes:
- Vendor claims non-payment
- Verify payment status
- Provide payment confirmation
- Resolve discrepancies

Stop Payments:
- Requested by vendor or company
- Fees may apply
- Requires authorization
- Process through bank

Payment Records:

Documentation:
- All payments documented
- Invoices and approvals retained
- Payment confirmations saved
- Audit trail maintained

Retention:
- Records kept for 7 years
- Digital storage preferred
- Accessible for audits
- Secure storage required

Reporting:

Payment Reports:
- Payments by vendor
- Payments by period
- Payment method summary
- Outstanding payments

Access:
- Finance: Full access
- Department managers: Department payments
- Executives: Summary reports

Security:

Payment Security:
- Segregation of duties
- Dual authorization for large amounts
- Regular reconciliation
- Fraud monitoring

Banking Security:
- Secure banking relationships
- Multi-factor authentication
- Regular account monitoring
- Immediate fraud reporting
