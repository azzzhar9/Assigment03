Budget Planning and Management Guidelines

Annual Budget Process:

Budget Calendar:
- Q3: Budget planning begins (July)
- Department heads submit budget requests (August)
- Finance consolidates and reviews (September)
- Executive team reviews and approves (October)
- Final budget communicated (November)
- Budget effective January 1st

Budget Categories:

1. Personnel Costs:
- Salaries and wages
- Benefits (health, retirement, etc.)
- Bonuses and incentives
- Training and development
- Contractor and consultant fees

2. Operating Expenses:
- Office rent and utilities
- Technology and software licenses
- Marketing and advertising
- Professional services
- Travel and entertainment
- Office supplies and equipment

3. Capital Expenditures:
- Equipment purchases over $5,000
- Software implementations
- Facility improvements
- Major infrastructure projects

Budget Request Process:

Department Budget Submission:
1. Review previous year actuals
2. Forecast next year requirements
3. Justify increases or new line items
4. Submit detailed budget request
5. Include supporting documentation
6. Present to Finance and executives

Budget Approval:
- Department budgets reviewed by Finance
- Variances from previous year explained
- Executive team makes final decisions
- Budgets may be adjusted during review
- Final approved budget is binding

Budget Management:

Monthly Reviews:
- Actual vs. budget comparison
- Variance analysis required for >10% differences
- Department heads review with Finance
- Corrective actions for over-budget items

Quarterly Forecasts:
- Update full-year forecast
- Adjust for known changes
- Identify risks and opportunities
- Present to executive team

Budget Modifications:

Mid-Year Adjustments:
- Budget transfers between line items (same department)
- Requires Finance approval for >$5,000
- New budget items require executive approval
- Budget increases rarely approved mid-year

Budget Variances:

Acceptable Variances:
- +/- 5% considered normal
- Seasonal variations expected
- One-time events may cause variances

Unacceptable Variances:
- Consistent over-budget spending
- Unexplained large variances
- Spending without budget allocation

Overspending Consequences:
- Department head meeting with Finance
- Spending freeze until resolved
- May impact future budget allocations
- Disciplinary action for policy violations

Budget Reporting:

Available Reports:
- Monthly budget vs. actual
- Year-to-date spending
- Forecast to year-end
- Department comparisons
- Category analysis

Access Levels:
- Department heads: Full department budget
- Managers: Team budget subset
- Finance: All budgets
- Executives: Summary and department details

Capital Budget:

Capital vs. Operating:
- Capital: Assets with useful life >1 year, cost >$5,000
- Operating: Day-to-day expenses

Capital Approval Process:
1. Business case required
2. ROI analysis for major items
3. Department head approval
4. Finance review
5. Executive approval for >$50,000
6. Procurement process

Spending Controls:

Purchase Approvals:
- Under $1,000: Manager approval
- $1,000-$5,000: Department head
- $5,000-$25,000: CFO
- Over $25,000: CEO

Budget Holds:
- Finance can place holds on budgets
- Requires explanation and resolution plan
- Holds removed after issues addressed

Best Practices:

1. Plan Ahead:
- Forecast needs early
- Build in contingency
- Consider seasonality

2. Monitor Regularly:
- Review spending weekly
- Catch issues early
- Adjust behavior proactively

3. Communicate:
- Keep Finance informed
- Explain variances
- Request help when needed

4. Be Strategic:
- Align spending with goals
- Prioritize high-impact items
- Eliminate waste
